The nightmare

Your booking system is a diary, a chat, and your memory.

Caterers, tent houses, decorators and photographers run peak season on three things — and all three fail at exactly the wrong moment.

The paper diary

Two families ask for 12 December. You write both on different pages, three weeks apart. Nobody notices until the tent truck is loaded.

The WhatsApp trail

The advance was confirmed in a chat that is now 400 messages deep. "Aapne kitna diya tha?" becomes an argument on the wedding morning.

Your own memory

You remember the wedding. You forget the balance was never collected. By the time you check, the guests have gone home and so has the money.

One clash. One forgotten balance. That is all it takes for a family to tell every relative not to book you.

The wedge

A double-booked date is not a scheduling error. It is the one mistake that ends a vendor’s name. Catch the clash the moment the booking is written — and every rupee of advance and balance stays on the record, not in an argument.

How it works

From enquiry to balance collected — in four steps.

  1. 1

    Write the booking

    Enter the event, date, party size and total. Takes less time than a WhatsApp reply.

  2. 2

    MandapBook checks the date

    The moment you save, it compares against every booking on that date and your daily capacity — and warns before a clash becomes a crisis.

  3. 3

    Log the advance

    Record what the family paid. The balance is calculated for you and shown against the event, so there is never a "how much did I give" fight.

  4. 4

    Reminders go out

    MandapBook queues a reminder for the balance due before the event, so you collect on time — not after everyone has left.

Questions

Do I need to be good with computers?

No. If you can send a WhatsApp message, you can write a booking in MandapBook. It runs in the browser on your phone — nothing to install.

What if two bookings really are fine on one date?

You set your daily capacity. MandapBook only warns when a date crosses it, and you can always confirm and go ahead. It advises — you decide.

Will it send messages to my clients automatically?

Reminders land in your outbox — advance due, balance due before the event — so you stay in control of what goes out and when. Nothing is sent behind your back.
